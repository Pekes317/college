Why is learning so important to successful transformation and what does “Transformation through Learning” mean? Further, how does an organization or an individual successfully create transformation? The one-page briefs we call “Tips for Successful Transformation” will explore these issues and others related to successfully transforming. Frequently, we will add a new installment that will further explain our approaches to helping both individuals and organizations transform. We hope you will find these short articles to be both informative and insightful and will revisit us at AlexisGill.com for new installments of “Tips for Successful Transformation."

First, let’s define transformation by exploring the difference between “Change” and “Transformation.” We get older. Getting older is a change, not a transformation. Some people evolve and become more complex, wiser and braver in their thinking. Evolving to a higher cognitive level of thinking is an example of transformation.   Does your organization need to transform? What about YOU personally?

Companies buy and sell different parts, fire and hire, ebb and flow - that is change. At Heritage web solutions we consider ourselves the change specialists because we understand change and how it affects companies and individuals. When a company evolves into a whole new organization with new rules of operations and new ways of working to meet a challenge such as a weaker economy, then we can say there has been a transformation. In our minds, transformation should and usually is “for the better”. It is a way of being that is more complex, more mature and more likely successful given new situations and changing times. Transformation, we believe, is the best means to deal with rapid and increasing change. Transformation is created and supported by learning processes either individual, or organizational. This is why we have chosen the theme “transformation through learning” to clarify our work.

We believe transformation is a core process for the success of any organization trying to survive and thrive in the new century. Without individuals in that organization who are equipped to focus on making deep and profound personal changes in who they are and how they think and work, organizations will not be able to adapt to continuous change in the marketplace. Therefore, organizational transformation, essential in the new marketplace, is not possible without personal transformation. This presents organizations with tremendous challenges and uncertain futures unless they can find new and effective ways to support personal growth and transformation in a broad segment of their staff. Our work is all about helping organization create the conditions and experiences needed for personal transformation and hence organizational transformation.
